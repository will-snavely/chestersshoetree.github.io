Our History

Grab a chair. Maybe a snack. This is the full, unabridged history of Chester's Shoe Tree, lovingly compiled by Chester himself, who insisted every year be included, even the boring ones.

1987 — The Founding

Chester Higgenbottom III planted the first shoe tree in his backyard on a dare. Nobody remembers the terms of the dare. What we do know is that by spring, the tree had somehow produced a single, slightly damp loafer, and a business was born. Chester took out a loan, rented a storefront on Sole Street, and Chester's Shoe Tree officially opened its doors on March 14th, 1987, to a crowd of exactly four people, two of whom were lost.

1991 — The Great Sneaker Expansion

Riding the high of a surprisingly successful loafer line, Chester expanded into sneakers, a decision he made almost entirely because he liked how the word "sneaker" sounded. The Ultra Bounce line debuted this year and, against all odds, actually bounced.

This was also the year of the Great Inventory Fire, which we are legally required to describe as "a warehousing incident" and which absolutely did happen, we just can't elaborate.

1996 — Sandal Summer

A hot summer, a bold idea, and frankly not enough market research led to the launch of Chester's Signature Sandal. Customers were divided. Chester was not. He wore them every single day for the rest of the year, including to a funeral, which he still says was "the right call, honestly."

2001 — The Boot Years Begin

Following a trip to a mountain town whose name Chester can never quite remember, he came back obsessed with rugged footwear. The Rugged Grove Boot line launched this year and remains, to this day, the only product line with a instruction manual, mostly because customers kept calling asking which end goes on the foot.

2005 — We Got A Computer

Chester's Shoe Tree purchased its first business computer in 2005, six years after most businesses did, and used it primarily to play a shoe-themed screensaver that Gary from accounting still insists was "very calming." Inventory would continue to be tracked on paper for another decade regardless.

2009 — The Almost-Website

An early attempt at a website was built this year, went live for exactly one afternoon, and was taken down after it was discovered to be, in Chester's words, "somehow worse than no website." No screenshots survive. We would like to believe this current website represents significant improvement. We would like to believe that.

2014 — The Loafer Renaissance

A resurgence of interest in the Classic Brown Loafer, the shoe that started it all, prompted Chester to rerelease the original 1987 design unchanged, down to the same slightly damp quality that nobody could ever explain or fully get rid of.

2018 — Grove Expansion

The backyard shoe grove was expanded from four trees to eleven trees, a 175% increase that Chester describes as "basically doubling," which is not how percentages work, but we let him have it.

2023 — The Big Sale

Chester's Shoe Tree ran its biggest clearance sale to date over Black Friday weekend. It was, by all accounts, a tremendous success, and it is a little strange that the countdown timer on our homepage still seems to think it's happening.

Today

Chester's Shoe Tree remains a family business, run by Chester, occasionally helped by Gary, and now represented online by this very website, which Chester has described as "mostly finished" for several years running. We thank you for reading this far. We know it was a lot. There was no reason for it to be this long. We're not sorry.
